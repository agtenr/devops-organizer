## Observation
Objective criterion O5 ("dependencies/blockers named or explicitly 'none'") declined an otherwise strong story (scored 90/100, all four judgment criteria Good) purely because the author did not write an explicit "no dependencies" line. This recurs specifically on foundational/root setup stories (this one is the first story under its parent, with genuinely nothing to depend on): authors treat "no dependencies" as self-evident and omit it, so the rubric's "silence is a miss" rule fails them on a technicality even when the story is plan-ready. The cause is a mismatch between how authors write greenfield/root stories and a criterion that demands an explicit negative declaration.
