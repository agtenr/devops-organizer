## Observation
Objective criterion O5 ("Dependencies/blockers are named or explicitly none; silence is a miss") was hard to apply cleanly. The story named its real dependencies (the Graph email service, the debug component) inline inside a "Rules" section as implementation instructions, not as an explicit dependency/blocker declaration. The rubric does not say whether such inline references satisfy "named" or whether an explicit declaration (or an explicit "none") is required, so the pass/fail call rested on the agent's reading rather than an objective test. Because O5 is a hard gate, this ambiguity is the difference between approve and decline on otherwise well-specified stories.
