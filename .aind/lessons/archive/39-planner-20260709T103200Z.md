## Observation
On the plan PR the planner posted five assumption threads each phrased as a recommendation followed by "Prefer the alternative?". The human's resolving replies were terse ("agreed", "Agree", "No agree with suggestion"), and because the questions were yes/no framed against the *alternative*, a bare affirmative was genuinely ambiguous — it could read as accepting either the recommendation or the alternative. The planner had to disambiguate out-of-band before it could safely fold the decisions in. Cause: open-question threads that pose a yes/no on the alternative invite one-word replies whose referent is unclear, whereas presenting the choice as an explicit either/or ("A or B?") would make a terse reply unambiguous.
