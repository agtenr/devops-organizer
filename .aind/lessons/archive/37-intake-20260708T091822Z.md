## Observation
Across stories 35, 36, and 37 the author declared dependencies in prose ("dependency on #30", "direct dependency on #36") and attached the referenced items as ADO **Related** links, never as **Predecessor** links. The dependency gate (aind-deps.sh) only resolves Predecessor links, so it returned DEPS_VERDICT: NONE for all three and never enforced sequencing. This is a real gap: #37 declares a direct dependency on #36, and #36 is not yet implemented, yet the gate could not catch it and the story was approved on objective criteria alone. The cause is a mismatch between how authors express dependencies here (prose + Related links) and the single link type the gate trusts (Predecessor). Objective criterion O5 is satisfied by naming a dependency in prose, so the rubric and the gate can disagree — a story can pass O5 while its real blocker stays invisible to the gate.
