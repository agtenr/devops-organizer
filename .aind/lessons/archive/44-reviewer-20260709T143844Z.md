## Observation
testing.md mandates that categorization fixtures be committed/tracked JSON so the Vitest suite is "hermetic and deterministic" ("the committed fixtures — not the raw .msg files — are what the tests import"), and the tracked fixtures README asserts the same. But .gitignore line 25 excludes src/services/categorization/__fixtures__/*.json by deliberate privacy design (comment: "derived from the real DevOps corpus, stay local, never committed"). The two directly contradict: in reality only README.md is tracked, all 18 fixtures (14 existing + 4 new) exist only locally, so the suite is unrunnable on a fresh clone and the fidelity-critical fixtures are unverifiable from a PR diff alone. This surfaced during story 44 as a review observation and forced a plan/rule-vs-codebase judgement call. The cause: the anonymisation convention (which exists precisely so fixtures CAN be committed safely) was added to testing.md/README without reconciling the older gitignore rule that assumed the fixtures still held real PII; with no CI configured, the local-only workflow masked the contradiction.
