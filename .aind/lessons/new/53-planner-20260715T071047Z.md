## Observation
While grounding the plan I hit a documentation drift inside the categorization fixtures: `src/services/categorization/__fixtures__/README.md` says "Tests read these committed JSON files" and a comment in `categorizationService.test.ts` says it loads "committed JSON fixtures", but the actual `.gitignore` ignores `src/services/categorization/__fixtures__/*.json` and `.claude/rules/testing.md` mandates the fixtures are gitignored, local-only, and regenerated from the `.msg` corpus on a fresh clone. The two in-code artifacts and the authoritative rule disagree on the single most load-bearing fact about the fixtures (are they in the PR or not). This matters because a coder trusting the "committed" wording could `git add` the personal-data-derived fixtures (a privacy leak) or assume a fresh clone/CI already has them. The cause is that the "committed" wording predates or was never reconciled with the gitignore + testing-rule decision that fixtures stay out of git.
